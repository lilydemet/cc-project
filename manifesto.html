AVOID THE SUBJECT
a sticker on a street-post in gastown offers the first rule for mapping negative space.
avoid the subject.
negative space is a concept that, in art,
refers to space surrounding the subject––
space that is necessary in order for the subject to exist
but which is not the intended focus of attention.
take a map of vancouver that serves navigational purposes, like google maps or
the paper ones you can take for free at waterfront station.
the subject of these maps are roads, followed by differently-shaded polygons which
signify the buildings, green space, and water which (according to the map) make up the city.
if roads are the subject then alleyways are relegated to negative space.
to render vancouver by mapping its alleyways is to avoid the subject, shifting focus to spaces in-between
and in so doing, creating a counter-cartography of and for new navigations.

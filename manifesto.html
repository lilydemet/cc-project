A MANIFESTO for mapping negative space


a Manifesto is a declaration.

you navigated to this page and are reading these words because I declared the document in which they are written to be html.

the Manifesto of this Manifesto is: <DOCTYPE html> (right click here and choose "inspect" to see what i mean).

a document's declaration allows a web browser (like google chrome, apple safari, or mozilla firefox) to read its contents and display it according to the rules of its language. if not for <DOCTYPE html>, what you are reading would not exist here.

just as a document declaration makes my html file legible to a web browser, my Manifesto For Mapping Negative Space provides me, the web (and urban) flâneur, a language with which to communicate what it is i do. it articulates a framework which brings form to an otherwise indefinite concept.

in what follows I provide directive, though no direction. wander and wonder as you will. go forth and glean. that's all im really doing.



a sticker on a lamppost in gastown offers the first directive for mapping negative space: AVOID THE SUBJECT.

negative space is a concept that, in art, refers to that which surrounds the subject––space that is necessary in order for the subject to exist but which is not the intended focus of attention.
in writing, negative space is whitespace. its all that the words are not. its in the margins and the distance between a period and capital letter. creative writing and poetry in particular engage (with) the page in ways that incorperate space into the creative work itself. the space left empty carries value beyond legibility. it gives room for the reader to sit with what words evoke. creative forms of writing are well suited to render encounters of deep mapping as they allow for juxtaposition and the dissolution of space/time linearity. Anachronisms and anachorisms allow multiple stories to be woven together.


SLOW DOWN
Day to day we use maps to find our way about. We use digital maps to find out when the next bus is coming and how long it will take me to get where we are to where we are going. But if there were no destination, what would the journey be like? Mapping negative space requires slowing down in order to be with the landscape rather than merely moving through it. Time allows for depth.

ITS ALL PART OF IT

Mapping Negative Space is a cartography of in-betweens––spaces, places, sensations, and processes that shape the conventionally intended object of focus though are seldom mapped in their own right. Because deep mapping is a counter cartographic praxis rather than a kind of counter map, what i have here digitized is an assemblage of articulations, a spatial bricolage of my gleaning.

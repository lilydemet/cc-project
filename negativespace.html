This from my initial project proposal:
My counter-cartography in its material form will be a 2D (if digital) or 3D (if physical) ‘basemap’ from which windows will be cut, gaps through which creative cartographies produced by mapping deeply will be explorable. I will render these pockets of affect through narratives, poetry, audio/visuals, and imaginative sketches. The form of my counter-cartography is opposite to that of a found poem: I am uncovering places on the existing page/basemap/space that together create their own poem/mapping/place. It plays with the concept of negative space, a term in art that refers to space surrounding the subject––space that is necessary in order for the subject to exist but which is not the intended focus of attention. (The view from Lions gate is spectacular, but what if I focused instead on the rumbling of cars and the rhythms that reverberate in my body? I dip my fingers in the ocean and feel the sonic throb of tanker engines.) My counter-cartography will be a lost poem, a collection of negative spaces in and around Vancouver that were encountered unintentionally, felt deeply, and which coalesce to form an affective map of this place I’ve begun to call home.

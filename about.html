i began constructing this space on a whim, knowing nothing of github but immensly inspirerd by the page of Sultana Zana (found here). The way they shared their mindscape inspired me to build out my own. however, i knew nothing of github and very little html. with the internet, though, one can teach themselves anything. i learned as i went, "making as/through thinking and thinking as/through making"––a practice fundemental to the creative humanities (van der Tuin and Verhoeff 2022, 2-3.)

this page began as a place to host my counter cartography/ies of negative space/s, though i soon realized my proof of concept was inextricable from the process that created (and is still creating) it. so i began to database my mind, moving between multiple scales until i had created a scaffolding for projects like Mapping Negative Space. the question of what platform to render my map with had been troubling me for it seemed all options that were ready-made would, by their UI and UX, define the structure of my map and how others could interact with it. throwing it all on github adds a level of transparency and interactivity that an arcgis storymap could not. i want to de-emphasize the product, instead situating it within the context of its process. with github, i get to build the space my maps and all the thinking leading up to it will inhabit. whats more, github commits make visible my construction process. go to this page and see its structure in html. alternatively, right click where you are and 'inspect' to get a side by side view of the page you are seeing and the page your web browser is reading. (you might have to click the little grey traingles to expand the elements.)

that all being said, my limited capacity means i cannot always make appear what i want. so i improvise, work around, and even change directions. too much right now is linear. you land on the index page where i have given you 2 choices: go to DEEP_MAPPING or FIELD_NOTES. from there on i provide a directory in the upper corner so you can 'go somewhere else.' however, it becomes difficult to remember where you are in relation to other places in the space, and thus how they build on one another. im not yet able to script dynamic content. i do with embedding files and hyperlinking words so that you can click away to another site.

this page is a work-in-progress as much as its content is my work-in-process. so, feel welcome to reach me through spacetime at ldemet@student.ubc.ca with questions, suggestions, and critique.
